⬢ Become a Drop

Rent out spare disk space and bandwidth from a browser tab. Earn streaming credits for every chunk you serve. One tap to start.

Your device will ask to create a passkey (Face ID / fingerprint / PIN). That's your entire setup.

What just happened?

1
Your passkey became your identity. Your earning address is mathematically derived from it — same address every time, on this device, and no secret is ever stored anywhere. Clear your browser data and the passkey re-derives the same identity.
2
You started receiving encrypted chunks. Pieces of videos and files, encrypted before they ever reached you. You cannot see what they are — and that's your protection: you host ciphertext, indistinguishable from random data.
3
You earn per chunk served. Payments arrive as signed IOUs (watermarks) that accumulate instantly, off-chain, with no fees. Cash them on-chain whenever you like.

Your earnings

Open the Drop dashboard any time: chunks stored, credits accumulated, lifetime claimed.

Claiming: when your accumulated credits pass the threshold, claim from the dashboard. Two ways:

• Relay (default): the Jet submits for you and covers the transaction fee — you need nothing but the passkey.
• Direct: submit yourself — needs a few cents of BNB once.

Before serving big volumes, the dashboard also shows the Jet's own balance — a Jet can only promise what it can pay.

Privacy & safety

✓
Fully anonymous. No registration, no email, no name. Your only footprint is an earning address and a connection to your Jet.
✓
Can't-know hosting. Chunks are encrypted before you get them. You can't read them, and nobody can make serving decisions based on content — including you.
✓
Outbound-only. Your browser dials out; nothing connects in. No ports, no domain, no server.
Tips for earning more

Stay online. Reliability is scored; reliable Drops are offered full price, flaky ones half. Install this page as an app (browser menu → "Add to Home Screen") and allow the wake-lock prompt so the tab isn't put to sleep.

Offer more storage. More chunks hosted → more chunks served.

Desktop > phone for uptime, but phones work.
