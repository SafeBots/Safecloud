▶ Watching on Safecloud

Streaming where creators keep 90%, nobody tracks you, and the video comes from people, not platforms.

1
Click the link you were given. The video loads in your browser — no app, no account, no sign-up.
2
If it asks for a passphrase, the sharer chose extra protection: the link alone can't unlock the video. Type the words they told you separately.
3
Press play. That's the whole tutorial.

Who pays?

Usually: the website you're on. Sites sponsor viewing the way web sites always have — you just watch. Your viewing is anonymous: the payment system sees the site, never you.

Some content asks you to pay directly — fractions of a cent per minute, in streaming credits (Safebux). If so, the player shows a balance and a "get credits" button. 90% of what you pay goes to the creator, enforced by the payment rail itself — the infrastructure literally cannot collect its 10% without paying the creator in the same transaction.

What makes this different

🔒
Encrypted end to end. The computers storing and delivering the video cannot watch it. Decryption happens only in your browser.
🕵️
Private by default. Sponsored viewing leaves no trace of you anywhere public. Even paying yourself, each video uses a separate payment identity.
🌐
No platform. The video is served by independent operators who earn per chunk they deliver — and can't see, censor, or cherry-pick what they carry.
Troubleshooting

Black screen on iPhone? Requires iOS 17+. Reload once — the first segments pre-load on second visit.

"Payment required"? The site's sponsorship may be exhausted — tap "get credits" or come back later.

Passphrase rejected? All four words, lowercase, single spaces.

Want to support the network with spare disk space? Become a Drop — it's one tap.
